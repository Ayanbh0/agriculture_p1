OKRA

Pre-Nursery

• Seed Selection: Use high-yielding, disease-free seeds from reliable sources. Hybrid seeds are often preferred for better yields.

• Seed Treatment: Treat seeds with fungicides like Thiram or Carbendazim (2-3 g/kg of seed) to prevent seed-borne diseases. Treating seeds with Rhizobium culture or Trichoderma helps in better germination and disease resistance.

• Soil Preparation: Loosen the soil through plowing and ensure it is free of clods, stones, and weeds. Prepare raised beds if direct sowing is not being practiced.

Nursery

• Unlike tomatoes, okra seeds are often directly sown into the field due to their large size and quick germination. However, in colder areas, nursery raising may be practiced.

• Seed Sowing:

- Sow seeds in small polythene bags or trays filled with a mixture of soil, sand, and compost.

- Keep the soil moist and provide partial shade until germination occurs (4-7 days).

• Transplanting:

- Seedlings are ready for transplanting within 2-3 weeks when they have 2-3 true leaves.

NPK Fertilizer and Soil Management

• Soil Requirements:

- Well-drained, sandy-loam to clay-loam soils are ideal. The pH should be between 6.0–6.8.

- Conduct soil testing before planting to adjust nutrient levels

• Fertilization:

- Basal Dose: Apply 10-15 tons of farmyard manure (FYM) along with NPK 60:40:30 kg/ha at land preparation.

- Top Dressing: Apply an additional 30 kg Nitrogen/ha in two split doses (after 3 weeks of sowing and at flowering stage).

- Supplement with micronutrients like Zinc and Boron for healthy growth and pod formation.

Growing Process

• Seed Sowing:

- Sow seeds directly in the field at a depth of 2-3 cm.

- Spacing:Sow seeds directly in the field at a depth of 2-3 cm.

- Use 2-3 seeds per hole and thin to the healthiest seedling after germination.

• Irrigation:

- Use 2-3 seeds per hole and thin to the healthiest seedling after germination.

- Water every 4-5 days during flowering and fruiting stages.

• Weeding: Regular weeding is necessary during the early stages. Use mulching (e.g., straw or plastic) to suppress weeds and retain soil moisture.

• Earthing Up: Perform light earthing-up at 30-40 days after sowing to provide root support.

Others

• Weather Considerations:

• Okra thrives in warm climates with temperatures ranging from 25°C to 30°C.

• It is highly sensitive to frost and low temperatures.

• Requires moderate rainfall (500-700 mm), but excessive moisture can cause root rot and fungal diseases.

• Pest and Disease Management:

• Pests:

- Jassids, Aphids, Whiteflies: Use Neem oil or systemic insecticides like Imidacloprid.

Fruit Borer: Spray Spinosad or use pheromone traps to monitor infestations.

• Diseases:

Powdery Mildew:Use Sulfur-based fungicides like Wettable Sulfur.

Yellow Vein Mosaic Virus (YVMV): Control whiteflies (vectors of YVMV) and use virus-resistant varieties.

Root Rot: Ensure proper soil drainage and crop rotation.

• Harvesting:

- Okra pods are ready for harvesting 45-60 days after sowing, depending on the variety.

- Harvest tender pods (8-10 cm in length) regularly to encourage further production.

- Avoid overripe pods as they become fibrous and inedible.

• Harvesting Frequency: Pods should be harvested every 2-3 days.

• Yield: Average yield is 8-10 tons/ha under good management practices.

• Popular Varieties:

• Open-Pollinated Varieties: Pusa Sawani, Arka Anamika, Parbhani Kranti.

• Hybrid Varieties: Mahyco 10, Shakti 108, US 7109, Kashi Kranti.
