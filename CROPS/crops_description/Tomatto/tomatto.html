TOMATTO

Pre-Nursery

• Seed Selection: Use healthy, disease-free, and high-yielding seeds. Seeds are typically obtained from certified suppliers.

• Seed Treatment: Before sowing, treat seeds with Fungicide like Thiram or Captan (2-3 g per kg of seed) to protect against seed-borne diseases.

• Soil Preparation: Prepare a small seedbed or trays with well-drained loamy soil. The soil should be mixed with organic manure and sterilized (either through solarization or chemicals) to eliminate pathogens.

Nursery

• Time for Nursery Raising: Depending on the variety, nursery seedlings are raised for 4-6 weeks.

• Seed Sowing: Sow seeds in rows at a spacing of 5-7 cm apart. Cover with a thin layer of soil or compost.

• Watering: Use a fine sprinkler to water seedlings regularly, ensuring moisture but avoiding waterlogging.

• Shading: Provide partial shading initially to protect tender seedlings from direct sunlight.

• Weeding: Remove weeds regularly to prevent competition for nutrients.

NPK Fertilizer and Soil Management

• Soil Requirements:

- Tomatoes prefer well-drained, loamy or sandy-loam soil with a pH of 6.0–7.0.

- Soil Testing is recommended before planting to determine nutrient levels.

• NPK Fertilization:

- Basal Dose: Apply NPK fertilizer at the rate of 100:50:50 kg/ha (Nitrogen, Phosphorus, Potassium).

- Top Dressing: Split Nitrogen application into two doses (once at planting and another after flowering).

- Incorporate organic matter like compost or farmyard manure (10-15 tons/ha) to improve soil fertility.

• Micronutrients:

- Supplement with Calcium and Magnesium to avoid deficiencies (like blossom-end rot). Use foliar sprays of Boron and Zinc if needed.

Growing Process

• Transplanting:

- Transplant seedlings 4-6 weeks after sowing when they reach 10-15 cm height.

- Spacing: Maintain 60 x 45 cm or 75 x 60 cm spacing depending on the variety.

• Irrigation:

- Provide water immediately after transplanting.

- Regular watering is essential during flowering and fruit formation stages.

- Avoid over-irrigation to prevent root rot.

• Staking/Support: Install stakes or trellises for tall tomato varieties to keep fruits off the ground and reduce pest/disease risks.

• Pruning: Prune side shoots (suckers) and old leaves to improve air circulation and fruit quality.

• Mulching: Apply mulch (straw or black plastic) to retain soil moisture, suppress weeds, and control soil temperature.

Others

• Weather Considerations:

• Tomatoes grow best in warm temperatures between 21°C to 27°C.

• Avoid frost-prone areas, as tomatoes are sensitive to low temperatures.

• Rainfall of 600–800 mm is ideal, but prolonged heavy rainfall can cause diseases and waterlogging.

• Pest and Disease Management.

• Pests:

- Aphids, Fruit Borers, Whiteflies: Use insecticides like Neem oil or chemical controls (e.g., Imidacloprid).

- Cutworms: Apply soil treatments before planting.

• Diseases:

- Early/Late Blight: Use resistant varieties and fungicides like Mancozeb.

- Wilt and Root Rot: Ensure proper drainage and crop rotation.

- Leaf Curl Virus: Control whiteflies, which are vectors of the virus.

• Harvesting:

• Tomatoes are ready for harvesting 70–90 days after transplanting, depending on the variety.

• Harvest at the mature green or pinkish-red stage, depending on market demand.

• Yield: Average yield is 30–50 tons/ha depending on cultivation practices and variety.

• Popular Varieties:

• Determinate (Bush-type): Pusa Ruby, Arka Meghali, Roma.

• Indeterminate (Vine-type): Arka Vikas, Arka Alok, Marglobe.

• Hybrid Varieties: Abhinav, Shaktiman, Rakshitha.
