PAPAYA

Pre-Nursery

• Seed Selection: Use high-quality, disease-free, and certified seeds from reliable suppliers. Hybrid seeds ensure better yield and disease resistance.

• Seed Treatment: Treat seeds with Thiram or Captan (2-3 g per kg of seed) to protect against seed-borne diseases.

• Soil Preparation: Use well-drained, fine-textured soil enriched with compost or well-decomposed farmyard manure (FYM). Sterilize the soil to eliminate soil-borne pathogens.

Nursery

• Time for Nursery Raising: Cabbage seedlings take 25-30 days to grow in the nursery before transplanting.

• Seed Sowing:

- Sow seeds in lines 5-7 cm apart.

- Cover lightly with fine soil or compost and water immediately.

• Watering: Keep the soil consistently moist but avoid waterlogging.

• Shading: Provide partial shade to protect seedlings from excessive sunlight or rain.

• Thinning: Thin out overcrowded seedlings to avoid competition.

• Transplant Readiness: Seedlings are ready for transplanting when they reach 4-5 true leaves or a height of 10-15 cm.

NPK Fertilizer and Soil Management

• Soil Requirements:

- Cabbage thrives in well-drained, loamy or sandy loam soil with a pH of 6.0–6.5.

- Conduct soil testing to determine nutrient requirements.

• NPK Fertilization:

- Basal Dose: Apply 120:80:60 kg/ha of Nitrogen (N), Phosphorus (P), and Potassium (K) at the time of land preparation.

- Top Dressing: Split Nitrogen into two doses:

First dose: 30 days after transplanting.

Second dose: At the early head formation stage.

• Organic Matter:

Add 15-20 tons/ha of farmyard manure (FYM) or compost before planting to improve soil fertility.

• Micronutrients:

- Supplement with Boron and Calcium to prevent deficiencies like tip burn and poor head formation.

Growing Process

• Land Preparation:

- Plow the field thoroughly to make the soil fine and friable.

- Incorporate organic manure and level the field.

• Irrigation:

- Transplant 25-30 day old seedlings into the main field.

Spacing: Maintain a spacing of 45 x 45 cm or 60 x 60 cm depending on the variety and head size.

• Staking/Support: Install stakes or trellises for tall tomato varieties to keep fruits off the ground and reduce pest/disease risks.

• Pruning: Prune side shoots (suckers) and old leaves to improve air circulation and fruit quality.

• Mulching: Apply mulch (straw or black plastic) to retain soil moisture, suppress weeds, and control soil temperature.

Others

• Weather Considerations:

• Cabbage grows best in cool temperatures between 15°C to 20°C.

• Cabbage is a cool-season crop and thrives in regions with moderate rainfall (500-700 mm).

• Pests:

Cabbage Worms, Aphids, Cutworms, Diamondback Moth: Use biological control (e.g., Bacillus thuringiensis) or insecticides like Neem oil or Spinosad.

• Diseases:

- Black Rot: Avoid waterlogged conditions and use resistant varieties.

- Clubroot: Practice crop rotation and apply lime to adjust soil pH.

- Downy Mildew: Control through fungicide sprays like Mancozeb.

• Harvesting:

Maturity Period: Cabbage matures in 75-120 days depending on the variety.

Harvesting Stage: Harvest when heads are firm and reach the desired size. Cut the heads with a sharp knife, leaving a few outer leaves for protection.

Yield: Average yield is 30-50 tons/ha under good management.
